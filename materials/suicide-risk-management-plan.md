# Suicide Risk Management Plan

> **Note:** This plan was written for the NYSPI/CUIMC and Northeastern sites.
> Update the clinical contacts and site references for Pitt before use.

## Assessment Schedule

At clinical research assessments (screening, 1-month follow-up), study staff ask adolescents whether they receive treatment (service use measure) and provide referrals to treatment.

During the assent/consent visit, study staff collect phone numbers for the participant, parent/guardian (for minors), and one emergency contact.

At each clinical research assessment, study staff administer the full C-SSRS:

- **Screening and 1-month follow-up:** Full C-SSRS. Youth meet suicide risk criteria if they report "yes" to recent ideation questions 4 or 5.
- **Day of MRI scan:** C-SSRS screener. Youth meet suicide risk criteria if they report "yes" to questions 4, 5, or 6.

If an assessment spans multiple days, administer the C-SSRS once.

## Risk Response Procedure

When study staff identify risk:

1. Contact the designated licensed clinical psychologist:
   - NYSPI/CUIMC: Dr. Auerbach or Dr. Durham (licensed in New York State)
   - Northeastern: Dr. Aude Henin (licensed in Massachusetts); Dr. Auerbach is also licensed in Massachusetts
2. A clinically licensed team member (master's degree or higher) speaks with the guardian and adolescent as early as feasible.
3. That team member conducts a clinical evaluation and discusses emergency care or community clinical resources as appropriate.
4. The team member logs the interaction as a case note containing:
   - (a) Clinical context (e.g., interview setting)
   - (b) Risk assessment results
   - (c) Summary of action plan
5. The PI or designated clinical coverage follows up with the guardian 48-72 hours later to confirm follow-through or help find resources.
